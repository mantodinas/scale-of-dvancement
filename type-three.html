Type III Civilization

Architects of the Galaxy and masters of the cosmic void.

The Galactic Tier

A Type III civilization has transitioned from mastering a single star to harnessing the energy of an entire galaxy. This represents energy consumption of approximately 1036 Watts.

To a Type III civilization, the galaxy is a single organism. They would likely utilize self-replicating spacecraft to colonize billions of star systems over millions of years, turning the entire Milky Way into a massive computational or industrial network.

Black Hole Engines

Extracting energy from the rotation of supermassive black holes (the Penrose Process) or capturing radiation from quasars.

Von Neumann Probes

Self-replicating machines that travel between stars, building new copies of themselves to map the galaxy exponentially.

Stellar Lifting

The ability to manipulate stars, removing material to extend their lifespan or harvesting heavy elements for construction.

Intergalactic Reach

Utilizing wormholes or ultra-advanced propulsion to begin the journey toward neighboring galaxies like Andromeda.

Detecting Type III

Astronomers search for Type III civilizations by looking for "Red Galaxies"—galaxies that are unusually bright in infrared but dim in visible light, suggesting that the starlight is being captured by billions of Dyson structures.
